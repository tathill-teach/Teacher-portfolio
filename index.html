Vision for Effective Teaching

I believe great teaching creates brave learners—children who feel safe to ask questions, make mistakes, and try again. My classroom is a place where every voice matters, where curiosity is celebrated, and where we learn together as a community.

Core Teaching Principles

I teach through purposeful routines, hands-on exploration, and student-led discovery. I meet each child where they are, celebrate progress over perfection, and build a classroom culture rooted in kindness, respect, and joy.

Primary Goals

My primary goal is to nurture confident, capable learners who believe in themselves. I strive to create an inclusive space where every child feels valued, where learning feels like play, and where small victories build big dreams for the future.

The Gradual Release Model

🚀 The Gradual Release Model

This model helps children transition from relying on adult support to working independently. Breaking learning into manageable steps with guided practice builds confidence and competence.

I Do: Model & Think Aloud

I demonstrate the task step-by-step while explaining my thinking aloud. This helps students understand both the process and the purpose behind each step.

We Do: Guided Practice

Together we practice the skill while I provide prompts, encouragement, and scaffolds before gradually releasing responsibility.

You Do: Independent Practice

Students independently apply the new skill while I observe, provide feedback, and celebrate their progress.

🎨 Multi-Sensory Instruction

Abstract concepts like letters, numbers, and sounds become meaningful when children engage multiple senses.

🧩 Manipulatives & Hands-On Tools

Students use blocks, cubes, counters, playdough, and other tactile materials to make learning concrete and engaging.

🎵 Visuals, Sounds & Movement

I incorporate songs, movement, visuals, finger plays, picture supports, and color-coded charts so every learner can connect with the lesson.

📚 Visual Schedules & Predictable Routines

Five-year-olds thrive when they know what to expect. Predictable routines reduce anxiety and create a calm, structured classroom.

📅 Visual Calendars & Schedules

I use picture schedules and visual icons so students can anticipate transitions and understand the sequence of the school day.

🔔 Gentle Cues & Sound Signals

Soft chimes, timers, and transition songs help children prepare for changes while maintaining a positive classroom atmosphere.

📚 Lesson Plan

Segmenting Spoken Sentences into Words

This Kindergarten phonological awareness lesson focuses on segmenting spoken sentences into individual words using finger counting, manipulatives, and interactive discussion.

🔔 Bell Ringer

Say: "Listen to this sentence: The dog runs fast." Now let's count the words together using our fingers. The (1) dog (2) runs (3) fast (4)

TEKS Standard

K.2.A.iii — Demonstrate phonological awareness by identifying the individual words in a spoken sentence.
